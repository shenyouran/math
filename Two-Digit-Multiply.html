A Kind of Two-Digit Multiplication

Introduction: There are 90 two-digit numbers, so there are 90^2=8,100 multiplication expressions (with two factors). Let's learn some easy ones.

1. Pick.

First, pick two two-digit numbers. The two numbers' tens-digit are the same number. The two numbers' ones-digits' sum is 10.

For example, 26*24.

Add one to the tens-digit. 2+1=3. Multiply 3 and 2, get 6. Then multiply the ones-digit. 6*4=24. Put them together and get 624.

2. Check with two ways.

(1) (30-4)*24=30*24-4*24=720-96=624
(2) 26*(20+4)=26*20+26*4=520+104=624
